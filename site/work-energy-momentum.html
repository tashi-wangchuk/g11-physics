Work, Energy & Momentum

Forces act over time and distance. By stepping back and looking at the beginning and the end of a process, we find quantities that never change — conservation laws that govern everything from colliding atoms to rockets and orbits.

1  2D Collisions & Momentum

When two objects collide in a closed system, the net external force is zero. This means their total momentum vector $\vec{P} = m_1\vec{v}_1 + m_2\vec{v}_2$ is conserved. Whether they bounce elastically or stick together inelastically, the momentum before and after the crash is identical. However, kinetic energy behaves differently: it is conserved in elastic collisions, but dissipates into heat and deformation in inelastic ones.

2  Conservation of Energy

Mechanical energy is the sum of kinetic energy (energy of motion) and potential energy (stored energy). If only conservative forces like gravity do work, mechanical energy is conserved: as height decreases, speed increases. If friction is present, some mechanical energy converts to thermal energy (heat), but the total energy remains constant.
