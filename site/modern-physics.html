1  The Photoelectric Effect

When light hits metal, it knocks out electrons. If light were a classical wave, any color could eject electrons if it were bright enough. But Einstein showed that light consists of packets called photons. An electron absorbs one photon: if the photon's energy ($E = hf$) is less than the metal's work function ($\Phi$), no electron escapes, no matter how bright the beam. If $E > \Phi$, electrons fly out instantly.

2  Wave-Particle Duality

Fire electrons one-by-one at a double slit. Each electron lands as a single point on the screen (particle nature). Yet, as we accumulate thousands of electrons, they organize into an interference pattern of bands (wave nature). Each electron passes through both slits as a probability wave, interfering with itself before collapsing to a point on the screen.
