Electricity & Magnetism

Electric charges create forces through fields. By placing static charges, running currents through circuits under Ohm's Law, and rotating magnetic fields, we see the dual nature of electromagnetism in action.

1  Electric Field Sandbox

Coulomb's Law states that positive and negative charges attract or repel each other with an inverse-square force. Across empty space, we say charges create an electric field. The net electric field vector at any point is the vector sum of the fields from each charge. Field vectors point away from positive charges and toward negative ones.

2  Circuits & Ohm's Law

Ohm's Law (V = IR) dictates how voltage from a battery pumps current through resistors. When resistors are connected in series, the current has only one path, increasing total resistance. In parallel, current splits across multiple paths, reducing equivalent resistance and allowing each bulb to burn at full battery voltage.
