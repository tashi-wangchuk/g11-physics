1  Reaction Time & Normal Distribution

Every measurement has uncertainty. If you time a pendulum, your reaction time adds random fluctuations. By repeating the measurement, we see a bell curve (normal distribution) form. The average (mean) is our best estimate. The standard deviation (σ) measures clicking precision, and the standard error of the mean (σm = σ/√N) measures how certain we are of that average.

2  Data Fitting & Least Squares

Scientific data is noisy. To extract a physical relationship, we fit a line (or curve) to the data. In the method of least squares, the "best" line is the one that minimizes the sum of squared residuals—the vertical distances between the points and the line. By minimizing this error, we find the true trend.
